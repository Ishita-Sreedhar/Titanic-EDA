# Titanic-EDA
An exploratory data analysis of the famous Titanic dataset, covering data cleaning, univariate and bivariate analysis, feature engineering, and survival prediction insights.
# About
This project performs an exploratory data analysis on the Titanic dataset, uncovering the key factors that influenced a passenger's chance of survival.
# Key Findings
Few key findings of this EDA are as listed below:
- Only 38% of passengers survived the Titanic disaster.
- Women had a 74% survival rate compared to just 19% for men.
- 1st class passengers survived at 63% vs 24% for 3rd class, showing a clear class divide.
- Passengers who paid a higher fare had a higher chance of survival, largely due to fare being linked to class.
- Children below the age of 10 had a higher survival rate, indicating they were prioritised for rescue.
- Solo travellers had a low survival rate, small families of 2-4 had the best survival rate, and large families of 5+ had poor survival.
